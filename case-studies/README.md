# Case Studies

## Overview
This folder contains a collection of smaller, topic-focused exercises completed throughout the bootcamp. While less extensive than the main projects, each case study demonstrates a specific analytical skill — from data cleaning and statistical exploration to experimentation, segmentation, and business decision-making. Together, they showcase breadth of capability across the data analytics workflow.

## Case Studies Breakdown

| Folder | Focus Area | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `data-cleaning/` | Data Collection & Cleaning | Identifying and fixing data quality issues — handling missing values, duplicates, inconsistent formats, and structuring raw data into an analysis-ready format. |
| `fraud-eda/` | Exploratory Data Analysis & Statistics | Exploring a fraud-related dataset to uncover patterns, distributions, and anomalies using statistical and visual analysis techniques. |
| `excel-analysis/` | Advanced Excel Analysis | Using pivot tables and charts in Excel to summarize and analyze data for quick, digestible business reporting. |
| `ab-testing/` | A/B Testing & Experimentation | Designing and evaluating a controlled experiment to determine the statistical significance of a business change before full rollout. |
| `customer-segmentation/` | Customer Segmentation & Decision Making | Grouping customers based on behavioral or transactional attributes to support targeted marketing and retention strategies. |
| `business-process-analysis/` | Business Process & Problem-Solving | Mapping a business process to identify bottlenecks, inefficiencies, and root causes, then proposing data-driven improvements. |
| `business-strategy-case/` | Business Case & Decision-Making Strategy | Analyzing a business case scenario and developing a strategic recommendation based on available data and business context. |

## Skills Demonstrated
- Data cleaning & preprocessing
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) & statistical thinking
- Excel-based reporting (pivot tables & charts)
- Experimental design (A/B Testing)
- Customer segmentation techniques
- Business process & root-cause analysis
- Strategic business case evaluation

## Note
Each sub-folder contains its own dataset, working file, and/or summary write-up. While these case studies are smaller in scope compared to the main projects, they reflect consistent practice across the core competencies expected of a data analyst — from technical execution to business-oriented thinking.
